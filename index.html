Methodology & sources

Scenario frameworks differ. Physical-climate pathways use CMIP6 SSP scenarios (SSP1-2.6 / SSP2-4.5 / SSP5-8.5); transition pathways use NGFS narratives (Net Zero 2050 / Delayed Transition / Current Policies). The Low/Intermediate/High labels align them conceptually only — the names are not interchangeable across the two modelling frameworks.

Definitions. Temperature anomaly and precipitation change are relative to the 1981–2010 baseline. "Extremely hot days" are days above 35 °C (national average). Uncertainty ribbons show the CMIP6 ensemble 10th–90th-percentile bounds. Physical outcomes are not recalculated from national investment choices, which would require an integrated climate model. Projection points are plotted at 20-year-window centres (2030, 2050, 2070, 2090).

Sources. Physical climate: World Bank Climate Change Knowledge Portal (CMIP6 multi-model ensemble; ERA5 for observed history). Transition pathways: NGFS Phase V scenarios; base-year emissions and investment from IEA and US EIA. Abatement options: IEA Net Zero Roadmap and China carbon-neutrality roadmap, IPCC AR6 WGIII, Rhodium Group, IEA Global Methane Tracker. Tipping points: Armstrong McKay, D.I. et al. (2022), Science 377, eabn7950. Transition-pathway trajectories between published anchor points are interpolated consistent with NGFS scenario logic.
